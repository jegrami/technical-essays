## Purpose of this series

This article series explains the basics of how computers and the internet work, in plain language, for anyone who uses them.

Most people learn computers the same way they learn to drive: you learn what button does what, but not what's happening under the hood. That works fine, until something goes wrong, or you want to understand *why* things work the way they do. This series is meant to fill in those gaps, to give you a mental picture of what's really going on when you turn on a device, open an app, save a file, load a webpage, send an email, etc.

This isn't tied to one brand of computer or one operating system. The underlying ideas are mostly the same whether you use Windows, macOS, Linux, an iPhone, or an Android phone. Where something works differently depending on your device, I point that out. Where a modern idea traces back to something older, I also mention that too, since a little history often makes the "why" easier to remember.

No prior technical knowledge is assumed. Any technical term will be explained in simple words the first time it's used, so you can read straight through without needing to look anything up.

This series is organized as a set of short, self-contained articles, grouped into numbered sections. You don't need to read it start to finish. Feel free to jump to whatever topic you're curious about. 

That said, later articles sometimes build on ideas from earlier ones, so if something doesn't quite click, it may help to go back a step.
